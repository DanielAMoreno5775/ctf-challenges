Challenge Name: Needs More Salt

Intended Category: Grab Bag

Credit To: Daniel Moreno

Description: 
As an auditor, you've been called in by a company to ensure that they are satisfying their compliance needs. The company sells peaches, blueberries, and tangerines with most of their traffic coming from Florida.

For the most part, everything looks good. However, they've been tracking user journeys on their website. For legal reasons, they aren't allowed to store IPv4 addresses in a reversible manner, but they need to be able to track each IPv4 address across pages. As such, they've come up with a program to anonymize IPv4 addresses. Demonstrate that it is insufficient to render the addresses irreversible.

Submit the most common IP address within the provided logs as `PCA{000.000.000.000}`

Hints:
* Here's a list of IPv4 address blocks in Florida: https://networksdb.io/ip-addresses-of/state-of-florida
* Can you predict how salts are generated?

Solution:
1. Decompile hash_algorithm.pyc with decompyle++ or [PyLingual](https://pylingual.io/)
2. Realize that the CSV gives you the date for each entry
3. Calculate the seed for each day
4. Determine the correct salt for each day
5. Check a website like [this](https://networksdb.io/ip-addresses-of/state-of-florida) to see the list of IP ranges in Florida
    - This is technically unnecessary since there are only 4.2 billion possible IPv4 addresses.
6. Use that information to construct a small wordlist
7. Crack all of the hashes using hashcat (hash mode 17400) or a Python program for each day
8. After identifying one day, the player may notice that all of the IP addresses come from the same block (160.130.0.0/16), allowing them to further shrink the wordlist
9. Count how many times each IP address appears in the logs

Flag: `PCA{160.130.5.238}`
